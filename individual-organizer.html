Notes about stock sales and cost basis▼

When stock is sold, you will usually receive Form 1099-B reporting the proceeds from the sale. However, your statement may not always provide the cost/basis information necessary to compute gain or loss. If the statement does not contain the cost/basis information, you must provide it.

Often, "transfers" of stock within a brokerage account are actually sales of one type of stock and purchase of another. Even if you did not receive any cash from the transaction, you may have taxable gain or loss.

If your stock dividends are automatically reinvested, the dividends will be taxable even though you did not receive any cash. When the stock is sold, the amount reinvested over the years is taken into account.

If you sold property other than stock, your taxable gain or loss will be determined by your cost/basis. The cost/basis is usually the original purchase price plus improvements (the cost of repairs and maintenance are not taken into account for cost/basis).
